About Me

Hey, you have now landed in Debabrota Basu's webpage.

I am delighted to join as a tenured faculty (ISFP) at the Scool team (previously called SequeL) of Inria Lille Nord Europe in France. My research aims to construct algorithms and systems for developing efficient, robust, private, and ethical learning machines that solve real-life problems. Methodlogically, my work blends statistics, machine learning, and optimization.

I have been a postdoctoral researcher at the Data Science and AI Division, Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Chalmers University of Technology.

I obtained my PhD in Computer Science from the Department of Computer Science, School of Computing, National University of Singapore. Stéphane Bressan and Pierre Senellart have advised my doctoral journey.

I earned a B.E. degree with Honours in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering from the Department of Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering, Jadavpur University in 2014.

Outside academia, I write poetry and plays, air my dissent through articles on scientific and social issues, and have acted in plays and cinema. If any of these provokes your curiosity, have a look at the Arts and Articles page.

News

  • Accepted paper in Privacy-preserving Machine Learning workshop (PPML-PriML 2020) at NeurIPS 2020.
    Our paper on "Privacy in Multi-armed Bandits: Fundamental Definitions and Lower Bounds on Regret" with Christos Dimitrakakis is selected for PPML-PriML 2020. The detailed version is available at: https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.12298.
  • Accepted paper in ICBINB workshop at NeurIPS 2020.
    Our paper on "Inferential Induction: A Novel Framework for Bayesian Reinforcement Learning" with Christos Dimitrakakis, Emilio Jorge, Hannes Eriksson, and Divya Grover is selected for ICBINB@NeurIPS. The full version is available at: https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.03098.
  • Accepted paper in Proceeding of Privacy Enhancing Technologies Symposium (PoPETS), 2021.
    Our paper on "Differential Privacy at Risk: BridgingRandomness and Privacy Budget" with Ashish Dandekar and Stephane Bressan is selected for Issue 1 of PoPETS, 2021. Check the final version here.
  • Preprint on Verifying Fairness using Stochastic SAT Solvers.
    We (with Bishwamittra Ghosh and Kuldeep S. Meel) have proposed a stochastic SAT based approach Justicia to verify fairness of machine learning models. It comes with both experimental scalability and theoretical error bound unlike the existing formal verifiers of group fairness. The preprint is available at: https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.06516.
  • Updated preprint on Regret Lower Bounds for Private Bandits.
    We have updated our work on regret lower bounds for differentially private bandits with two new results. Now, we completed the table with two new minimax regret bounds for global DP in bandits.
